Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Solar panel installation is the process of setting up photovoltaic (PV) panels on rooftops, ground-mounted structures, or other suitable locations to capture sunlight and convert it into electricity.

Solar panels work by converting sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) cells. PV cells, typically made of silicon, absorb sunlight, causing electrons in the material to become energized. These energized electrons flow through the cell, generating direct current (DC) electricity. An inverter converts the DC electricity into alternating current (AC), which powers homes and businesses. The electricity is either used immediately, stored in batteries, or sent to the grid for later use.

Most homes can accommodate solar panels as long as they have adequate sunlight exposure. Ideally, the roof or property should face south with minimal shading. However, east- and west-facing roofs are also viable options, though they may generate slightly less electricity.

The most common option for homes and businesses, include flat or sloped roofs. Ground-Mounted Systems are Ideal for properties with ample open land and limited roof space. Carports can have Solar panels installed on parking structures to provide shade and generate power. Building Facades can also Panels integrated into the walls of buildings for energy generation and aesthetics. And Off-Grid Locations: Remote areas without access to traditional electricity grids, such as farms or cabins.

The cost of solar panels in Nigeria varies depending on the system size, quality, and installation requirements. On average: Small systems for basic appliances cost around ₦200,000 to ₦500,000. Medium systems for powering households cost between ₦1,000,000 to ₦3,000,000. Larger systems for businesses or industrial use can cost ₦5,000,000 or more. These prices include panels, inverters, batteries, and installation. While the initial cost is high, solar panels reduce electricity bills and provide long-term savings.

Solar panels typically last 25 to 30 years or more. Over their lifespan, their efficiency may gradually decrease, but most panels still produce around 80% of their original output after 25 years. It depends on quality, proper setup and maintenace.
