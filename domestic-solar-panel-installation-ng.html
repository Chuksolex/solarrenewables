Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

A residential solar system, often called a home or rooftop solar system, is a setup of solar panels installed on a residential property to harness sunlight and generate electricity for household use.

A residential solar system uses solar panels with photovoltaic (PV) cells to convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity. This DC electricity is then transformed into alternating current (AC) electricity by an inverter, making it suitable for powering household appliances. Any excess electricity generated can be fed into the grid or stored in batteries for future use.

Lower Electricity Bills: Solar energy significantly reduces or eliminates power costs, offering lasting savings. Eco-Friendly Energy: Solar power is renewable and reduces reliance on fossil fuels for a cleaner planet. Financial Benefits: You may qualify for tax credits, rebates, or incentives depending on your location. Increased Home Value: Solar installations can boost your property's resale appeal and value. .

The cost varies depending on system size, location, equipment quality, and installation. Request quotes from multiple providers to find the best fit for your needs.

The cost of solar panels in Nigeria varies depending on the system size, quality, and installation requirements. On average: Small systems for basic appliances cost around ₦200,000 to ₦500,000. Medium systems for powering households cost between ₦1,000,000 to ₦3,000,000. Larger systems for businesses or industrial use can cost ₦5,000,000 or more. These prices include panels, inverters, batteries, and installation. While the initial cost is high, solar panels reduce electricity bills and provide long-term savings.

The installation timeline varies based on system complexity and property size. Typically, it takes anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

Solar panels need minimal maintenance, with occasional cleaning and checks for shading issues. Inverter maintenance and system performance monitoring are also important. Many solar providers offer warranties and maintenance plans to keep the system running smoothly.

Many homes are well-suited for solar panel installation. Key factors like roof orientation, angle, shading, and structural integrity are evaluated by a solar professional during a site assessment to determine the viability of installing a solar system.

The ability to use solar power during an outage depends on your solar system type. Grid-tied systems without battery storage cannot operate during a power outage due to safety protocols. However, solar systems with battery backup, equipped with island mode functionality, or fully off-grid systems, can provide power even when the grid is down.

Solar panels are built to be durable and can last 25 to 30 years or more with proper maintenance. Inverters typically have a lifespan of 10 to 15 years and may need to be replaced during the system’s lifetime.
